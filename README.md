# HR Employee Attrition SQL Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes an HR Employee Attrition dataset using MySQL to identify factors contributing to employee turnover.

## Business Questions

1. Which departments have the highest employee attrition?
2. Does overtime increase employee attrition?
3. Which job roles earn the highest salaries?
4. Which age group has the highest employee attrition?
5. Does job satisfaction influence employee attrition?

## SQL Skills Demonstrated

- SELECT
- WHERE
- GROUP BY
- ORDER BY
- CASE
- COUNT()
- SUM()
- AVG()
- ROUND()
- Aggregate Functions

## Key Findings

- Research & Development recorded the highest attrition.
- Employees working overtime had a significantly higher attrition rate.
- Managers earned the highest average monthly income.
- Employees aged 30–39 experienced the highest attrition.
- Lower job satisfaction was associated with higher attrition.

## Dataset

IBM HR Employee Attrition DatasetInsights

1) Employee attrition is concentrated in specific departments.
* Some departments experience significantly higher employee turnover than others, indicating that workforce retention challenges are not evenly distributed across the organization.

2) Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the company.
* The analysis shows a higher attrition rate among employees who regularly work overtime, suggesting that excessive workload or work-life imbalance may contribute to employee turnover.

3) Salary varies considerably across job roles.
* Managerial and specialized roles generally earn higher average monthly incomes than operational or entry-level positions, reflecting differences in responsibilities and seniority.

4) Attrition is more common within certain age groups.
* Mid-career employees (or whichever age group your query identifies) experience the highest number of resignations, highlighting a key demographic for retention initiatives.

5) Job satisfaction appears to influence employee retention.
* Employees with lower job satisfaction levels are more likely to leave the organization, indicating that employee engagement and workplace experience are important drivers of retention.

Recommendations

1) Develop department-specific retention strategies.
* Conduct employee surveys and exit interviews in departments with the highest attrition to identify the root causes and implement targeted retention programs.
2) Reduce excessive overtime.
* Review workload distribution, improve resource planning, and encourage a healthier work-life balance to reduce employee burnout and turnover.
4) Review compensation and career progression.
* Evaluate whether employees in lower-paying roles have competitive salaries and clear opportunities for promotion and professional development.
5) Focus on high-risk employee groups.
* Provide mentorship, career development plans, and regular performance discussions for the age groups with the highest attrition to improve engagement and retention.
6) Improve employee satisfaction.
* Strengthen recognition programs, enhance communication between managers and employees, provide learning opportunities, and regularly measure employee satisfaction to address concerns proactively.
